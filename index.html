What Heidi does

Notes, letters, summaries and follow-ups off the clinician's plate.

Most NZ teams start with Heidi's ambient scribe, listening in the background of the consult and producing structured, EHR-ready notes the clinician reviews and signs off. From there, Heidi can also handle patient calls and follow-ups, and answer clinical questions with cited sources.

  • Heidi Scribe: ambient documentation, 110+ languages including te reo Māori
  • Heidi Comms: AI patient calls, reminders and self-scheduling (launched Feb 2026)
  • Heidi Evidence: cited, source-backed clinical answers at the point of care

Common questions

The questions we hear most.

Is Heidi safe to use in consultations?

Heidi listens during the consult to produce a structured note. Audio is processed in real time and not retained as a recording. The clinician reviews and signs off the note before it goes anywhere. Heidi was reviewed and endorsed by Health NZ's NAIAEAG. Read more about safety & privacy.

Do patients need to consent?

Consent is part of every consult. We provide a short script, patient explainer, waiting-room poster and reception FAQ so the conversation is easy. Patients can decline at any time without affecting their care.

Will Heidi work with our PMS or EHR?

Heidi works with the major NZ practice management systems either through copy-paste or, where available, deeper integration. We confirm what's possible for your specific setup during the discovery call. See current integrations.

How long does rollout take?

Two weeks for a typical GP practice; a few days for solo allied-health practitioners; 6–12 weeks per service for hospital deployments. We can start with a small pilot if that suits your team.

What does it cost?

Heidi has plans for individuals, small teams, and larger organisations. Hendrix's job is to help you choose the right fit, factor in training and rollout, and walk through procurement. Get NZ pricing guidance.
