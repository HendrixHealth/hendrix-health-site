Consent & recording

Patients consent. Recordings are not retained.

Heidi listens during the consult to produce a structured note. The audio is processed in real time and not retained as a recording. The clinician sees the note before it goes anywhere. They review, edit and decide what stays in the chart.

  • Patient consent is part of every consult; we provide the script
  • Audio is transcribed in real time, not kept as a recording
  • The clinician reviews and edits the note before saving
  • Patients can decline at any time without affecting their care

Sample consent script

"Today I'd like to use a tool called Heidi that listens during our consult and helps me write the notes. It doesn't keep a recording, and I'll review what it produces before anything is saved. Is that okay with you?"

Adapt to your tone. Patients almost always say yes, and reception teams who are briefed in advance get fewer follow-up questions.

Data handling

How consultation data moves, and where it stops.

The plain-English version. We provide the full technical brief during procurement, including a privacy impact assessment template.

During the consult

Real-time transcription, no stored recording.

Audio is transcribed as the consult happens. Heidi does not retain the audio file once the note is generated.

In transit & at rest

Encrypted end-to-end. Least-privilege access.

Encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest. Internal access is least-privilege and audited. De-identification is applied before AI processing where applicable.

In your chart

The clinician decides what's saved.

Heidi presents a note. The clinician reviews, edits, and pushes it to your PMS or chart. Nothing is auto-saved into the patient record without that step.

For the full technical brief (data residency configuration, retention policy, sub-processors, de-identification approach and audit posture), request the Heidi data-handling pack during procurement. Ask for the pack.

Clinical responsibility

The clinician stays in charge. Always.

Heidi is a documentation and communication tool. It is not a diagnostic system. The clinician reviews every note, edits as needed, and signs it off. Clinical decisions remain with the clinician, supported not replaced.

  • The clinician reviews every note before it enters the chart
  • Notes can be edited, regenerated, or discarded entirely
  • Heidi flags content as AI-generated; the clinician's edits are theirs
  • Decision-support content (Heidi Evidence) cites its source; the clinician judges
  • Clinicians can switch Heidi off mid-consult; the patient relationship comes first

Te Tiriti in practice

What "Te Tiriti-friendly" looks like in a real rollout.

Less about a values statement; more about specific operational decisions we make in every implementation we run.

Te reo Māori in the consult

Heidi transcribes consults in te reo Māori and bilingual settings, supporting clinicians who consult in te reo and Māori patients in their own language.

Patient material reviewed for plain language

The patient-facing pack is written for plain-language readability and reviewed for cultural appropriateness, not a literal translation of a vendor leaflet.

Data sovereignty in onboarding

Data residency, retention and consent are surfaced as decisions in onboarding, not buried in a contract. Provider organisations supporting Māori communities get a tailored data-governance walkthrough.

Training shaped by Project Ikuna

Co-founder Kalolo's work on the MAIA award-winning Māori and Pacific digital upskilling programme shapes how we design training for every team, not only those serving Māori communities.

Equity in adoption metrics

Where you measure adoption, we encourage measuring across patient demographics, not just clinician adoption. The goal is a tool that serves your whole population, not the average one.

Public framing

The Heidi–Hendrix partnership has been described publicly as "Te Tiriti-friendly AI". We treat that as a standard to keep meeting, not a label that's already earned.
