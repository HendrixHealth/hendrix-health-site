Implementation & training

What a Heidi rollout actually looks like.

Adopting an AI scribe is a workflow change, not a software install. We run a five-stage rollout for every NZ team, covering clinical, operational and patient-facing needs, so adoption is safe, calm and sticks past month one.

The Hendrix rollout

Five stages. Run end-to-end by your client partner.

Every clinic is different, but the shape of a successful rollout isn't. This is what we do for a typical NZ practice or hospital service.

  1. Discover

    A short call to understand your team, consult types, PMS/EHR, patient communication needs, and what success looks like for you.

    Week 0 · 30 min
  2. Prepare

    We set up templates, brief reception, share patient-facing material, line up an internal champion, and walk through the privacy approach.

    Week 1 · async + 1 huddle
  3. Train

    Role-specific training for clinicians, practice managers, and reception. Live, recorded, and reusable for new joiners.

    Week 1–2 · 60–90 min/role
  4. Go live

    A nominated client partner is on hand for the first week, solving snags, adjusting templates and helping the team build confidence.

    Week 2 · live support
  5. Optimise

    Check-ins at 30 / 60 / 90 days. Template refinement, usage review, new-feature briefings, and follow-up training as the team grows.

    Ongoing · quarterly

A real rollout is rarely linear.

Our five stages are a frame, not a fixed schedule. Some practices want to start with a single GP and grow from there. Some hospital services run all five stages in parallel across multiple sites. Your client partner adjusts the cadence to your team, not the other way around.

Training that lifts the whole team

Role-specific. Recorded. Reusable for new joiners.

We don't drop a single 60-minute session on the team and hope. Each role gets training pitched at the work they actually do.

Clinicians

From first consult to comfortable in 1 week.

Live walkthrough, a practice consult with a real recording, and a starter template library tailored to your specialty. Quick reference card for the desk. We follow up in week two to refine notes and templates.

Practice managers

The operational pieces, sorted.

Setup of templates and team accounts, patient-facing material for the waiting room and website, a briefing for reception, an internal champion, and clear answers for the privacy questions you'll be asked.

Reception & front-of-house

Consistent answers to patient questions.

A short briefing and a one-pager so anyone who picks up the phone can confidently explain what Heidi is, that consent matters, and that recordings aren't kept. Patients notice when reception is at ease.

CIO / clinical leads (enterprise)

Governance, security, and adoption metrics.

Procurement support, privacy impact assessment templates, a deployment plan tailored to your sites, and a reporting cadence that gives clinical and digital leadership the same view of how things are landing.

By sector

What rollout looks like for your kind of team.

Three illustrative shapes of rollout. Your client partner will tailor the work to your practice.

General practice

A 4-GP clinic, live in two weeks.

Discovery call, templates set up against your PMS, training across two evenings, and a client partner on hand during the first week of go-live. Reception is briefed before any patient asks.

Typical timeline: 14 days · Roles trained: 4 clinicians · 2 PMs · 3 reception

Hospital service / ED

A multi-site service, in phases.

Pilot one site, capture lessons, then phase across the wider service with a clinical lead per site. Training delivered in shift-friendly blocks. Reporting back to digital and clinical leadership monthly.

Typical timeline: 6–12 weeks per service · Largest deployment: Health NZ ED rollout, all 38 public EDs

Allied health & specialist

Single practitioners and small teams.

For physio, audiology, psychology, dental and similar: a one-on-one walkthrough, templates tuned to your consult shape, and a 30-day follow-up. Often live in under a week.

Typical timeline: 3–7 days · Sectors covered: physio · OT · audiology · psychology · vets · dental

After you reach out

What happens next.

No high-pressure sales call. A short conversation, a clear plan, and a recommendation you can take back to your team.

  1. We reply within one working day.

    From your local client partner, usually faster.

  2. A 20-minute readiness conversation.

    We ask about your team, systems, consult types, and what success would look like for you. No demo unless you want one.

  3. A short demo, if helpful.

    Heidi running on a real consult, in your specialty. Followed by a Q&A with the people who'd actually use it.

  4. A written rollout recommendation.

    Plan, training mix, timeline, patient-communication pack, pricing pathway. Written so your team can review without you in the room.

  5. You decide. We support, in or out.

    If now isn't the right time, we'll say so. If it is, your client partner runs the rollout end-to-end.

Common questions

Rollout questions we hear most.

How long does a typical rollout take?

For a single GP practice, two weeks is typical from kick-off to confident daily use. Hospital services usually run in phases over 6–12 weeks per service. Allied health and specialist solo practitioners can be live within a few days.

Can we start with a small pilot?

We recommend it for most teams. Starting with one or two willing clinicians, capturing what works and rolling forward from there is usually faster and calmer than a big-bang launch.

What training do you actually run?

Live sessions tailored to each role: clinicians get a hands-on walkthrough with a practice consult; practice managers get setup, templates, and patient-comms; reception gets a short briefing and a one-pager. Sessions are recorded so new joiners can self-onboard.

Will Heidi work with our PMS / EHR?

Heidi works with the major NZ practice management systems either through copy-paste or, where available, deeper integration. We confirm what's possible for your specific setup during the discovery call. See current integrations.

What ongoing support do we get?

Check-ins at 30, 60, and 90 days, then quarterly. Template refinement and follow-up training as your team changes. New Heidi features are walked through with you when they land. Your client partner stays the same person over time.

Who handles patient communication?

You do, but we make it easy. We provide a waiting-room poster, a plain-language patient explainer, an FAQ for reception, and a short consent script clinicians can adapt. Read more on Safety & privacy.

What does it cost?

Heidi has plans for individuals, small teams, and larger organisations. Hendrix's job is to help you choose the right fit, factor in training and rollout, and walk through procurement if your organisation needs it. Get NZ pricing guidance.
